Reading a foraging colony with the tools of information

A stigmergic ant colony choosing between two food sources — pheromone deposited, pheromone decayed, ants recruited by what earlier ants left behind. The amber layer is the colony itself. The cyan layer is what we can measure about it: how much causal structure its collective state actually carries, in bits, as it crosses from disordered search into locked-in exploitation. Every number here is computed live and validated against known limits. What this instrument does not claim is stated plainly at the bottom — that boundary is the point.

What this instrument does and does not claim

Validated — trust these

  • The effective-information core is checked against known limits: a deterministic permutation returns exactly log₂N bits; a fully random matrix returns exactly 0.
  • Determinism, degeneracy, and their identity EI=(det−deg)·log₂N hold to numerical precision.
  • The colony dynamics are a faithful Deneubourg recruitment model with pheromone decay and exploration noise — the order→disorder transition across β and ρ is real and reproducible.
  • The phase portrait is a 5-seed mean over an independent parameter sweep, not a single run.

Not claimed — and why

  • This does not measure "emergent collective intelligence." Detecting genuine causal emergence (macro-scale beating micro-scale) requires a validated positive control — a synthetic system known to be emergent that the pipeline correctly flags.
  • Under this uniform-intervention measure, symmetric collective systems tend toward exact scale-invariance: the coarse-grained view gains determinism but loses states in equal measure, netting ≈0. Building a clean positive control is harder than it looks, and until one passes, no emergence claim is honest.
  • So the instrument reports what it can stand behind — the information content of the colony's state at a single scale — and leaves the cross-scale claim open. That restraint is deliberate.
